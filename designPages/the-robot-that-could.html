The Robot That Could

— One weekend, one mission.

The robot that could was conceptualized two days before its final presentation, built the day before, and perfected the day of. Despite this, it completed the final course for Berkeley's DESINV 22 class admirably. Its durrable construction meant that desipte being run over and pushed off a ramp by larger robots, it was able to complete the course quickly and while remaining intact. This project would not have been possible without the incredible design work for my partner Sarah Katz.

Features.

  • Magnetically attached front for easy access to to internals.
  • Internal LEDs that pulse to communicate the power at each motor.
  • Striking, simple, attractive design.
  • Easy to use Bluetooth controls.
